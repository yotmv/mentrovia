> This is general small business guidance, not legal, tax, payroll, or accounting advice. Verify requirements with the appropriate government agency and review decisions with a qualified professional before filing or relying on them.

A new local business doesn't need a marketing department — it needs to be findable, credible, and mentioned. Here's a 30-day plan that costs mostly time.

## Week 1 — Be findable

- **Claim your Google Business Profile.** For local search ("mower repair near me"), this single free listing outworks everything else. Complete every field, add real photos, set service areas and hours.
- **Get the basics consistent everywhere:** same business name (matching your filings), phone, and service area on your website or landing page, social profiles, and directories. One page with services, prices or starting-prices, photos, and a phone number beats a delayed ten-page site.
- Set up a way to **ask for and display reviews** — the link Google gives you, sent by text after every job.

## Week 2 — Be credible

- Post 3–5 pieces of proof: before/after photos, a completed job, a customer quote (with permission).
- Write your **one-paragraph pitch** (from your brand kit) as the pinned post / About text everywhere.
- Print something physical if your trade works that way: cards, door hangers, a yard sign for job sites.

## Week 3 — Be mentioned

- Tell the 20 people most likely to refer you — past customers, neighbors, complementary businesses (the plumber who doesn't do what you do). A direct "I've started X, here's my card, I'd appreciate referrals" outperforms any ad this month.
- Join the local groups where your customers ask for recommendations (neighborhood apps, community Facebook groups) and answer questions helpfully *before* pitching.

## Week 4 — Spend a little, measure honestly

- If weeks 1–3 are done, a **small paid test** makes sense: boost your best proof post, or run one narrowly-targeted local ad with a single offer and a phone-call goal.
- Track one number per channel: calls, messages, or form fills. Ask every new customer "how did you find us?" and write it down.

## The rule that keeps you sane

Do the free, compounding things (profile, reviews, referrals) *before* the paid, evaporating things. Ads amplify a findable, credible business; they can't create one.
