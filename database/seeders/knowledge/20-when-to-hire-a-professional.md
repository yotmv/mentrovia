> This is general small business guidance, not legal, tax, payroll, or accounting advice. Verify requirements with the appropriate government agency and review decisions with a qualified professional before filing or relying on them.

Mentrovia can organize what you need to think about; it cannot replace the four professionals below. Here's when each one earns their fee.

## Bookkeeper — hire earliest, cheapest leverage

**When:** transactions outgrow your weekly 30 minutes, you're behind more than a quarter, or you simply won't do it.
**What they do:** categorize, reconcile, keep the books current, hand clean reports to you monthly and your CPA yearly.
**Not their job:** tax strategy, legal advice.

## CPA — the highest-ROI relationship most owners underuse

**When:** at formation (structure and election advice), the first year with real profit, before an S election, whenever payroll or multi-owner complexity appears — and quarterly once profitable.
**What they do:** tax returns, estimated-tax planning, entity/election fit, franchise tax filings, reasonable-compensation guidance, audit response.
**Buying tip:** a CPA consulted in November can change your tax bill; one hired in April can only report it.

## Attorney — episodic, but non-negotiable at the episodes

**When:** entity formation questions with real liability at stake, operating/partnership agreements between co-owners, leases, customer contracts you'll reuse, trademark protection, disputes, and any letter that scares you.
**What they do:** documents that hold up, and judgment about risks you can't see yet.
**Buying tip:** an hour before signing costs less than a lawsuit after.

## Payroll provider — the moment employee #1 starts

**When:** first hire, full stop. Deposit schedules, withholding, TWC filings, W-2s, and new-hire reporting are automated by every mainstream provider for a modest monthly fee, and penalties for DIY mistakes exceed years of those fees.
**What they don't cover:** classification judgment (employee vs. contractor) and compensation strategy — those are CPA/attorney questions.

## How to choose any of them

Ask what they charge and how (hourly, monthly, per-filing), who their typical client is (you want "businesses like mine"), how they prefer to communicate, and what they need from you monthly. Then actually send it monthly.
