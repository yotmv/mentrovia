> This is general small business guidance, not legal, tax, payroll, or accounting advice. Verify requirements with the appropriate government agency and review decisions with a qualified professional before filing or relying on them.

If Mentrovia gives one piece of near-universal advice, it's this: **open a dedicated business bank account and stop running business money through your personal account.**

## Why separation matters

- **Bookkeeping:** with a dedicated account, your bank feed *is* your business activity. Mixed accounts mean sorting personal groceries from business supplies forever.
- **Taxes:** deductions are far easier to substantiate when business expenses live in a business account with receipts to match.
- **Liability:** if you formed an LLC or corporation for liability separation, commingling funds is one of the classic ways that separation gets attacked.
- **Credibility:** paying vendors and getting paid under the business name looks professional and simplifies disputes.

## What banks usually ask for

Exact requirements vary by bank and structure, but expect to bring:

- Personal identification for each owner/signer.
- **EIN** confirmation letter (or SSN for some sole proprietors — though an EIN is usually better).
- **Formation documents** (certificate of formation) for LLCs and corporations.
- **Assumed name certificate** if operating under a DBA.
- **Operating agreement** or ownership details for multi-member entities.

## A simple account structure that works

1. **Business checking** — all income in, all expenses out.
2. **Tax reserve savings** — move a fixed percentage of every deposit; this funds estimated taxes and, if you collect it, sales tax. Collected sales tax is the state's money — treat the reserve as untouchable.
3. **Owner account (personal)** — pay yourself deliberately (owner draw or payroll, depending on structure) instead of spending from the business account.

Add a dedicated business card for expenses so statements stay clean, and consider a payroll reserve when you hire.

## Banking hygiene checklist

- No personal spending from the business account — pay yourself first, then spend personally.
- Record every owner contribution and draw in your books.
- Reconcile the account monthly.
- Keep merchant/payment processor payouts flowing into business checking, not personal accounts.
