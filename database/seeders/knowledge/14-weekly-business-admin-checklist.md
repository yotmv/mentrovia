Fifteen to thirty minutes, same day every week. The point isn't perfection — it's that nothing silently ages for a month.

## Money in

- **Review accounts receivable.** Who owes you? Send or re-send invoices; nudge anything past due while it's still awkward-free.
- **Record the week's income** if anything happens outside your bank feed (cash, checks to deposit).

## Money out

- **Categorize new transactions** in your bookkeeping software while you still remember what they were.
- **Upload receipts** — photograph the paper ones, forward the email ones.
- **Review unpaid bills** so vendor payments go out on your schedule, not in a panic.

## Money position

- **Check the cash balance** against what's due out in the next two weeks (rent, payroll, tax set-asides). Cash surprises are almost always visible a week early if someone looks.
- **Glance at sales tax collected** (if you have a permit): is the right amount accumulating in your books or reserve account?

## People and promises

- **Review payroll hours** if you have employees — catch timekeeping gaps before the pay run.
- **Log business mileage** for the week while trips are still memorable.
- **Scan open customer/vendor issues** — one unanswered complaint is a task; five are a reputation problem.

## Deadlines

- **Look two weeks ahead** at your task calendar: filings, renewals, estimated tax dates, license expirations. The weekly glance is what makes the monthly and quarterly checklists painless.

Do this weekly and the monthly close becomes a 30-minute confirmation instead of a forensic reconstruction.
