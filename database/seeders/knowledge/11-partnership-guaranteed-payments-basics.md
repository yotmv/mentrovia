In a partnership (or a multi-member LLC taxed as one), partners generally aren't employees of their own partnership and don't take W-2 wages from it. Partner compensation happens through profit allocations, draws, and — when the partnership wants salary-like certainty — **guaranteed payments**.

## The three ways money reaches a partner

**Profit allocation.** Each partner is taxed on their share of partnership profit (per the partnership agreement) whether or not cash is distributed. This is the baseline: the tax bill follows the allocation, not the cash.

**Draws / distributions.** Cash actually paid out to partners against their share. Draws themselves are generally not separately taxed — they're the cash side of already-taxed allocations — but they matter for basis and fairness between partners.

**Guaranteed payments.** Fixed amounts paid to a partner for services (or use of capital) **regardless of whether the partnership makes a profit** — the closest thing partnership tax law has to a salary. They're generally deductible by the partnership, taxable to the receiving partner, and generally subject to self-employment tax.

## When guaranteed payments make sense

- One partner works full-time in the business while others invested capital — the working partner gets a guaranteed payment for labor before profits split.
- Partners want predictable monthly compensation independent of seasonal profit swings.
- The partnership agreement needs a clean way to compensate unequal effort without redrawing ownership percentages.

## What to get right

- **Put it in the partnership agreement.** Amounts, conditions, and how they interact with profit splits belong in writing, agreed before the money flows.
- **Expect self-employment tax.** Working partners generally owe SE tax on guaranteed payments and their share of trade income — quarterly estimated taxes apply.
- **Record them distinctly** in the books; they appear separately on the partnership return and each partner's K-1.

## Questions to ask a CPA

- Should the working partner be compensated by guaranteed payment, a bigger profit share, or both?
- How do our draws interact with each partner's basis?
- Are our quarterly estimated taxes right given allocations plus guaranteed payments?
