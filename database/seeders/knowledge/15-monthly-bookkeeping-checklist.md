> This is general small business guidance, not legal, tax, payroll, or accounting advice. Verify requirements with the appropriate government agency and review decisions with a qualified professional before filing or relying on them.

Once a month, close the books on the month that just ended. An hour, maybe two — dramatically less if the weekly habit is holding.

## Reconcile

- **Bank accounts:** every line in the bank statement matches a transaction in your books, and the ending balances agree. This is the single most valuable bookkeeping habit that exists.
- **Credit cards:** same drill for the business card.
- Investigate anything unrecognized *now* — fraud, duplicate charges, and forgotten subscriptions all surface here.

## Review the statements

- **Profit & loss:** compare with last month and your gut. Revenue where you expected? Any expense category quietly ballooning?
- **Balance sheet:** cash, what customers owe you (A/R), what you owe (A/P and cards), and your sales-tax liability should all look plausible.

## Taxes

- **Move the tax reserve:** transfer your set-aside percentage of the month's profit to the tax savings account.
- **Sales tax:** if the Comptroller has you on monthly filing, file and pay by the due date. Quarterly and yearly filers: confirm the liability accruing in your books matches what's parked in the reserve.

## People

- **Payroll reports** (if applicable): gross, taxes, and filings from your provider all ran correctly.
- **Contractor payments:** monthly totals by payee stay current, so 1099 season is trivial.

## Housekeeping

- **Owner pay:** record the month's draws/salary/distributions deliberately.
- **Subscriptions and recurring costs:** cancel what the business stopped using.
- **Insurance and coverage:** anything changed (new vehicle, new service line, first employee) that your policies don't reflect?
- **Marketing pulse:** one honest look at what last month's spend produced.

Close the month, then leave it closed — resist editing past months except through your CPA's guidance.
