> This is general small business guidance, not legal, tax, payroll, or accounting advice. Verify requirements with the appropriate government agency and review decisions with a qualified professional before filing or relying on them.

Once a year — ideally split between early January (filing season prep) and your slow season (strategic review) — sweep everything the weekly/monthly/quarterly rhythm doesn't cover.

## January window (deadline-driven)

- **1099-NEC forms** to contractors and the IRS — the due date lands early in the new year; confirm the current date and threshold with the IRS.
- **W-2s** to employees (your payroll provider generates these — verify, don't assume).
- **Prepare the books for tax filing:** final reconciliations, December close, and a clean P&L and balance sheet package to your CPA. The earlier your CPA has it, the better your filing options.

## State compliance

- **Texas franchise tax report** (plus the Public/Ownership Information Report) for taxable entities — confirm the current due date on the Comptroller's site and calendar it.
- **Registered agent and addresses:** still correct with the Secretary of State? Agents who moved or resigned are how entities miss lawsuits and notices.
- **Assumed name certificates:** check remaining term; renew before expiration.
- **Licenses and permits:** industry, city, and county — renew whatever expires this year.

## Insurance and risk

- Review every policy (liability, property, vehicle, workers' comp decision) against what the business looks like *now*, not when you bought it.

## Strategy (the slow-season half)

- **Entity and tax-election fit:** did this year's profit level change the sole-prop-vs-LLC or S-election math? Ask your CPA.
- **Owner compensation strategy** for the coming year.
- **Banking and credit:** rates, fees, and whether a line of credit should exist before you need it.
- **Annual budget** for the coming year, however rough.
- **Brand, website, and marketing plan refresh** — one deliberate pass per year.

Calendar every dated item the moment you confirm the current deadline — future-you is unreliable.
