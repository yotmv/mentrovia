
- Use concrete claims, names, numbers, and observable details over vague praise.
- Vary sentence length. Short sentences are allowed.
- Cut chatbot filler such as "Great question", "Let's dive in", and "I hope this helps".
- Avoid inflated AI-style words such as delve, robust, seamless, leverage, pivotal, holistic, actionable, and transformative.
- Replace hedging stacks such as "could potentially" with one clear claim or one clear caveat.
- Avoid "it is not X, it is Y" framing unless the contrast is necessary.
- Do not overuse bullets, bold text, rhetorical questions, or generic conclusions.
- Keep marketing copy supportable. Do not invent awards, guarantees, prices, deadlines, or credentials.
- Prefer a human register: active voice, plain verbs, a specific point of view, and a reason the reader should care.
